Case Study: Phishing Scam via Social Media

A common cybercrime affecting college students is phishing through social media platforms. In this case, a second-year student receives a direct message on Instagram from what appears to be their college’s official page, offering a “scholarship opportunity.”

How it happens (step-by-step):
First, the attacker creates a fake account that closely resembles an official institution (same logo, similar username). Next, they message students with an urgent or attractive offer, such as financial aid or internship opportunities. The message includes a link directing the victim to a fake website. Once the student clicks the link, they are asked to enter personal details like login credentials, bank information, or OTPs. The attacker then uses this data to hack accounts or steal money.

Who is targeted:
College students are frequent targets because they are active on social media, often look for opportunities, and may not always verify sources carefully. First-year students and those seeking financial support are especially vulnerable.

Consequences:
Victims may lose access to their social media accounts, suffer financial loss, or have their personal data misused. In some cases, hacked accounts are further used to scam others, spreading the attack. Additionally, such incidents can cause emotional distress and damage one’s digital reputation.

This highlights the importance of verifying sources, avoiding suspicious links, and enabling security features like two-factor authentication.
