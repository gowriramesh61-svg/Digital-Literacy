This Digital Literacy project is designed to develop essential digital skills required for students in today’s technology-driven world. Based on the role of a Student Digital Ambassador, the project focuses on helping peers understand and effectively use digital tools, platforms, and communication methods in a responsible and professional manner.

The project consists of five key tasks that cover different aspects of digital literacy. These include creating an awareness infographic, building a professional digital portfolio, exploring coding and collaboration platforms, understanding email etiquette, and studying cybercrime along with its prevention. Each task combines practical application with reflection, allowing for a deeper understanding of digital practices.

All the work is organized into a GitHub repository, which serves as a central platform to showcase the completed tasks and learning outcomes. Overall, the project aims to enhance technical skills, promote safe online behavior, and prepare students for academic and professional digital environments.

Module Overview
Module 1
This module focused on developing practical skills using various digital tools and software applications. It introduced platforms like Canva, Google Slides, and other design tools to create visual content effectively. The emphasis was on understanding user-friendly interfaces and applying creativity to communicate ideas clearly through digital media.
Module 2
This module highlighted the importance of creating a professional online presence. It involved setting up and managing profiles on platforms such as GitHub and LinkedIn. The goal was to help students showcase their skills, achievements, and academic journey, which can be useful for future internships and career opportunities.
Module 3
In this module, students explored coding practice platforms like HackerRank or CodeChef and learned the basics of problem-solving. It also introduced collaborative tools such as Google Forms and Google Sheets, emphasizing teamwork, data collection, and efficient sharing of information.
Module 4
This module focused on professional communication in the digital space. It covered how to write structured and formal emails, maintain proper tone, and follow etiquette rules. It also addressed responsible behavior on social media, including what to post, how to interact, and maintaining a positive digital image.
Module 5
This module created awareness about various types of cybercrimes such as phishing, identity theft, and online payment fraud. It emphasized understanding how these crimes occur, their impact, and preventive measures. Special attention was given to financial safety, including secure use of UPI and awareness of reporting mechanisms.
